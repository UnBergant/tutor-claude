# Spanish Grammar Curriculum (Castellano) -- CEFR A1-C2

Reference framework for Celestia AI tutor. Based on the Instituto Cervantes Plan Curricular, DELE exam requirements, and CEFR descriptors. All content targets **Spanish from Spain (Castellano)** with Peninsular-specific features marked explicitly.

---

## How to Use This Document

- **Assessment engine**: use topic lists to identify what a student knows and where the gaps are.
- **Placement**: match demonstrated knowledge against level boundaries to assign an initial CEFR level.
- **Program generation**: build personalized modules by selecting topics the student has not yet mastered at their current level.
- **Progression logic**: topics within each level are ordered by pedagogical dependency -- earlier topics are prerequisites for later ones.

## Level A1 -- Breakthrough / Acceso

**Goal**: Produce and understand simple sentences about immediate needs, introductions, and concrete familiar topics using basic present-tense structures.

### Grammar Topics (ordered by progression)

1. **Noun gender and number**
   - Masculine/feminine patterns (-o/-a, -e, -ión, -dad, -ista)
   - Regular plural formation (-s, -es)
   - Invariable nouns (el/la crisis, el/la artista)

2. **Definite and indefinite articles**
   - el, la, los, las / un, una, unos, unas
   - Contractions: al, del
   - Basic distribution rules (with/without proper nouns)

3. **Subject pronouns and the tú/usted distinction**
   - yo, tú, él/ella, nosotros/nosotras, **vosotros/vosotras**, ellos/ellas
   - usted/ustedes (formal) vs. tú/**vosotros** (informal) -- **Spain-specific**: vosotros is the everyday informal plural, unlike Latin America where ustedes covers both
   - When to omit subject pronouns

4. **Present indicative: regular verbs**
   - -ar (hablar), -er (comer), -ir (vivir) full paradigms
   - **Vosotros forms** (-áis, -éis, -ís) introduced from day one

5. **Present indicative: core irregular verbs**
   - ser, estar, ir, tener, hacer, poder, querer, saber, decir, venir, salir, poner
   - Stem-changing verbs (e>ie: pensar, querer; o>ue: poder, dormir; e>i: pedir)

6. **Ser vs. estar**
   - ser: identity, profession, origin, time, material, possession
   - estar: location, temporary states, estar + gerund
   - Adjectives that change meaning (ser listo vs. estar listo)

7. **Hay vs. estar**
   - hay + indefinite (hay un banco)
   - estar + definite (el banco está en la calle Mayor)

8. **Demonstratives**
   - este/ese/aquel system (three-way spatial deixis -- **Spain-specific**: aquel is actively used in Spain more than in many Latin American varieties)
   - Gender/number agreement

9. **Possessives (unstressed)**
   - mi, tu, su, nuestro/a, **vuestro/a**, su
   - **vuestro/a/os/as** -- **Spain-specific**, not used in Latin American Spanish

10. **Basic adjective agreement and position**
    - Gender/number concord with noun
    - Default postnominal position
    - bueno/malo/grande apocopation (buen, mal, gran)

11. **Cardinal and ordinal numbers**
    - Cardinals 0-1000
    - Ordinals 1st-10th (primero, segundo...)
    - Apocopation: primer, tercer

12. **Basic quantifiers**
    - mucho/a/os/as, poco/a/os/as, bastante(s), demasiado/a/os/as
    - todo/a/os/as + article
    - algo, nada, alguien, nadie, algún/alguno, ningún/ninguno

13. **Question words (interrogativos)**
    - qué, quién/quiénes, cuál/cuáles, dónde, cómo, cuándo, cuánto/a/os/as, por qué

14. **Basic prepositions**
    - a, de, en, con, por, para, sin, entre, hasta, desde, hacia
    - Core uses: a (direction, IO marker), de (origin, possession), en (location), por/para basic distinction

15. **Negation**
    - no before verb
    - Double negation: no...nada, no...nadie, no...nunca, no...tampoco

16. **Estar + gerund (present progressive)**
    - Regular gerund formation (-ando, -iendo)
    - Irregular gerunds (leyendo, durmiendo, diciendo)
    - Ongoing actions

17. **Ir a + infinitive (near future)**
    - Future intention/plans

18. **Basic verbal periphrases**
    - tener que + infinitive (obligation)
    - hay que + infinitive (impersonal obligation)
    - poder + infinitive (ability/permission)
    - querer + infinitive (desire)
    - acabar de + infinitive (just did)
    - empezar a + infinitive (begin)
    - volver a + infinitive (do again)

19. **Gustar-type verbs**
    - gustar, encantar, interesar, molestar, doler
    - OI + verb in 3rd person (me gusta / me gustan)

20. **Reflexive verbs (basic)**
    - llamarse, levantarse, acostarse, ducharse
    - Reflexive pronoun placement (before conjugated verb)

21. **Imperative (affirmative tú and vosotros)**
    - Regular forms: habla, come, escribe / hablad, comed, escribid
    - Core irregulars: ven, pon, sal, haz, di, ten, ve, sé
    - **Vosotros imperative** (-ad, -ed, -id) -- **Spain-specific**

22. **Basic sentence structure**
    - SVO word order
    - Declarative, interrogative, exclamative
    - Inversion in questions (optional)

23. **Basic connectors**
    - y/e, o/u, pero, porque, también, tampoco, entonces

24. **Pretérito perfecto (present perfect)**
    - haber + past participle
    - Regular participles (-ado, -ido) and core irregulars (hecho, dicho, puesto, visto, escrito, vuelto, abierto, roto, muerto)
    - **Spain-specific usage**: in Spain, pretérito perfecto is used for actions in a time period that includes "now" (hoy, esta semana, este año), unlike many Latin American varieties where pretérito indefinido is preferred in all past contexts
    - Temporal markers: hoy, esta mañana, este año, ya, todavía no, alguna vez, nunca

### Spain-Specific Notes for A1
- **Vosotros** is not optional -- it is the standard informal plural and must be taught from the start
- **Distinción** awareness: in written exercises, c/z (before e/i) vs. s represent different sounds in Castellano (/θ/ vs. /s/). Students should be aware of this even if pronunciation drills come later
- The pretérito perfecto / pretérito indefinido split follows Peninsular norms (perfecto for "today-timeframe")

## Level A2 -- Waystage / Plataforma

**Goal**: Handle routine situations, narrate simple past events, describe habits and plans. Begin to connect sentences into short paragraphs.

### Grammar Topics (ordered by progression)

1. **Pretérito indefinido (simple past)**
   - Regular forms: -ar (-é, -aste, -ó, -amos, -asteis, -aron), -er/-ir (-í, -iste, -ió, -imos, -isteis, -ieron)
   - **Vosotros forms** (-asteis, -isteis) -- **Spain-specific**
   - Key irregulars: ser/ir (fui), estar (estuve), tener (tuve), hacer (hice/hizo), poder (pude), poner (puse), saber (supe), querer (quise), venir (vine), decir (dije), traer (traje), dar (di), ver (vi)
   - Stem-changing -ir verbs in 3rd person (durmió, pidió, sintió)
   - Temporal markers: ayer, la semana pasada, el año pasado, en 2020, hace dos días

2. **Pretérito perfecto vs. pretérito indefinido**
   - **Spain-specific contrast**: perfecto = time period still open (hoy, esta semana); indefinido = closed time period (ayer, el año pasado)
   - This distinction is a hallmark of Peninsular Spanish and must be drilled thoroughly

3. **Pretérito imperfecto (imperfect)**
   - Regular forms: -ar (-aba), -er/-ir (-ía)
   - Only three irregulars: ser (era), ir (iba), ver (veía)
   - Uses: habitual past, descriptions in the past, background actions, age/time in the past
   - Imperfecto vs. indefinido: background vs. foreground, description vs. event

4. **Combining past tenses**
   - Imperfecto (setting/context) + indefinido (event): "Llovía cuando salí"
   - Imperfecto (habitual) vs. indefinido (one-time): "Siempre iba al parque / Un día fui al parque"
   - Three-way contrast with perfecto

5. **Future simple (futuro simple)**
   - Regular formation: infinitive + -é, -ás, -á, -emos, -éis, -án
   - **Vosotros form** (-éis) -- **Spain-specific**
   - Irregular stems: tendr-, pondr-, vendr-, saldr-, podr-, sabr-, querr-, habr-, har-, dir-
   - Uses: predictions, promises, plans

6. **Conditional simple (condicional simple)**
   - Formation: infinitive + -ía, -ías, -ía, -íamos, -íais, -ían
   - Same irregular stems as future
   - Uses: polite requests (querría, podría), wishes (me gustaría), advice (deberías), hypothetical situations

7. **Direct and indirect object pronouns**
   - OD: me, te, lo/la, nos, os, los/las
   - OI: me, te, le, nos, os, les
   - **Leísmo de persona** -- **Spain-specific**: le used instead of lo for masculine singular person ("Le vi ayer" = "I saw him yesterday"). Accepted by the RAE for masculine singular persons. Students should recognize and produce this
   - Pronoun placement: before conjugated verb, after infinitive/gerund/imperative (enclitic)

8. **Combining OD + OI pronouns**
   - Order: OI + OD (me lo, te la, nos los...)
   - se lo/se la/se los/se las (le/les becomes se before lo/la/los/las)
   - Enclitic combinations: dámelo, díselo

9. **Imperative: negative forms**
   - Negative imperative uses subjunctive forms (no hables, no comáis)
   - **Vosotros negative imperative** (no habléis, no comáis, no escribáis) -- **Spain-specific**
   - Pronoun placement: proclitic in negative (no me lo digas), enclitic in affirmative (dímelo)

10. **Imperative: usted/ustedes**
    - Uses subjunctive forms: hable, hablen; coma, coman
    - Irregulars follow subjunctive patterns

11. **Possessives (stressed / tonic)**
    - mío/a, tuyo/a, suyo/a, nuestro/a, **vuestro/a**, suyo/a
    - Postnominal: un amigo mío, la casa es tuya
    - With article: el mío, la tuya

12. **Comparatives and superlatives**
    - más/menos...que, tan...como, tanto/a/os/as...como
    - Irregular comparatives: mejor, peor, mayor, menor
    - Superlative: el/la más + adj + de; -ísimo/a

13. **Relative pronoun que**
    - Restrictive relative clauses: "El libro que leí", "La chica que vive aquí"
    - Prepositional: "La persona con la que hablé"

14. **Preposition refinement**
    - por vs. para (expanded: cause vs. purpose, duration vs. deadline, means vs. recipient)
    - Verbs with fixed prepositions: depender de, pensar en, soñar con, acordarse de

15. **Adverbs of manner, time, frequency**
    - Formation with -mente (lentamente, fácilmente)
    - ya, todavía, aún, siempre, nunca, a veces, a menudo, de vez en cuando

16. **Conditional sentences type 1 (real/possible)**
    - si + presente indicativo, + presente/futuro/imperativo
    - "Si llueve, no salimos" / "Si llueve, no saldremos" / "Si llueve, quédate en casa"

17. **Basic uses of the subjunctive (present subjunctive only -- introduction)**
    - Formation: regular and key irregulars (sea, esté, tenga, haga, vaya, sepa, dé, haya)
    - **Vosotros subjunctive** (habléis, comáis, escribáis) -- **Spain-specific**
    - Uses limited to: querer que + subjunctive, esperar que, es necesario que, es importante que
    - Imperative connection: negative imperative = present subjunctive

18. **Estar + participle (resultative states)**
    - La puerta está abierta, estoy cansado
    - Distinction from passive

19. **Impersonal constructions**
    - se + 3rd person (se habla español, se venden pisos)
    - hay que, es necesario, es posible

20. **Connectors (expanded)**
    - Cause: porque, como, es que
    - Contrast: pero, sin embargo, aunque (+ indicative)
    - Consequence: por eso, entonces, así que
    - Time: cuando, mientras, antes de, después de

### Spain-Specific Notes for A2
- **Vosotros** forms expand across all new tenses (indefinido -asteis/-isteis, futuro -éis, condicional -íais, subjunctive -éis)
- **Leísmo de persona** is actively taught as standard Peninsular usage
- Past tense distribution follows Peninsular norms strictly

## Level B1 -- Threshold / Umbral

**Goal**: Express opinions, narrate experiences, describe hopes and plans, handle most travel situations. Produce connected text on familiar topics. Begin using the subjunctive productively.

### Grammar Topics (ordered by progression)

1. **Present subjunctive: systematic treatment**
   - Complete conjugation (all persons including vosotros)
   - Irregular stems: quepa, ponga, salga, tenga, venga, diga, haga, oiga, conozca, traduzca, construya
   - Stem-changing: piense/pensemos, pueda/podamos, pida/pidamos, duerma/durmamos, sienta/sintamos

2. **Subjunctive in noun clauses (oraciones sustantivas)**
   - Desire/will: querer que, desear que, esperar que, preferir que, pedir que, necesitar que
   - Emotion/reaction: me alegra que, me molesta que, siento que, me sorprende que, me encanta que, es una pena que
   - Doubt/denial: no creo que, dudo que, no me parece que, no es verdad que
   - Impersonal expressions: es necesario que, es importante que, es posible que, es probable que, es mejor que, está bien que
   - Indicative vs. subjunctive contrast: "Creo que tiene razón" vs. "No creo que tenga razón"

3. **Subjunctive in relative clauses**
   - Indefinite/unknown antecedent: "Busco un piso que tenga terraza" (vs. "Conozco un piso que tiene terraza")
   - Negative antecedent: "No hay nadie que sepa la respuesta"

4. **Subjunctive in adverbial clauses**
   - Purpose: para que, a fin de que
   - Time (future reference): cuando + subjunctive ("Cuando llegues, llámame"), antes de que, hasta que, en cuanto, tan pronto como
   - Time (past/habitual reference): cuando + indicative ("Cuando llego a casa, ceno")
   - Concession (hypothetical): aunque + subjunctive ("Aunque llueva, iré")
   - Concession (factual): aunque + indicative ("Aunque llueve, voy")

5. **Ojalá (que) + subjunctive**
   - Present subjunctive: wish for present/future ("Ojalá apruebe el examen")
   - Introduction to imperfect subjunctive with ojalá (unrealizable wish -- preview)

6. **Conditional sentences type 2 (unreal present/future)**
   - si + pretérito imperfecto de subjuntivo, + condicional simple
   - "Si tuviera dinero, viajaría a Japón"
   - Imperfect subjunctive formation: -ra forms (hablara, comiera, viviera) and -se forms (hablase, comiese, viviese)
   - **Spain-specific**: both -ra and -se forms are used in Spain; -se is more common in Spain than in Latin America where -ra dominates almost exclusively

7. **Pretérito imperfecto de subjuntivo**
   - Formation from 3rd person plural indefinido stem + -ra/-se endings
   - All irregulars follow this rule (tuvie-ra, pudie-ra, hicie-ra, dije-ra, fué-ra)
   - Uses beyond conditionals: past desire ("Quería que vinieras"), past emotion ("Me alegré de que aprobaras"), polite requests ("Quisiera un café")

8. **Pretérito pluscuamperfecto de indicativo**
   - había + past participle
   - Anteriority in past: "Cuando llegué, ya se había ido"
   - Reported experience: "Nunca había visto algo así"

9. **Futuro perfecto (future perfect)**
   - habré + past participle
   - Anteriority to future: "Cuando llegues, ya habré terminado"
   - **Probability in past** (Spain-specific emphasis): "Habrán salido temprano" = "They must have left early"

10. **Future and conditional for probability/conjecture**
    - Future: probability in present ("Serán las tres" = "It must be about three")
    - Conditional: probability in past ("Serían las tres cuando llegó" = "It was probably about three when he arrived")
    - **Spain-specific**: this usage is very productive in Peninsular Spanish and should be drilled

11. **Reported speech (estilo indirecto)**
    - Present > imperfect: "Dice que viene" > "Dijo que venía"
    - Perfecto > pluscuamperfecto: "Ha dicho que ha llegado" > "Dijo que había llegado"
    - Future > conditional: "Dice que vendrá" > "Dijo que vendría"
    - Imperative > imperfect subjunctive: "Ven" > "Me dijo que viniera/viniese"
    - Shifts in demonstratives, adverbs, possessives

12. **Passive voice**
    - ser + participle (passive periphrastic): "El libro fue escrito por Cervantes"
    - Passive reflexive (pasiva refleja): "Se venden pisos" -- more common in spoken Spanish
    - Contrast: "fue construido" (agentive) vs. "se construyó" (impersonal)

13. **Verbal periphrases (expanded)**
    - Modal: deber + inf (obligation), deber de + inf (supposition), soler + inf (habit)
    - Aspectual: seguir/continuar + gerund (continuation), dejar de + inf (cessation), ponerse a + inf (inception), estar a punto de + inf (imminence), llevar + gerund (duration)
    - Resultative: llevar + participle ("Llevo leídos tres capítulos")

14. **Expanded relative pronouns**
    - el/la/los/las que: "La persona con la que hablé"
    - quien/quienes (with/without antecedent): "Quien quiera, que venga"
    - donde, cuando, como as relative adverbs
    - lo que: "No entiendo lo que dices"

15. **Ser and estar with adjectives (expanded)**
    - Systematic contrast: ser (inherent quality) vs. estar (state/change/result)
    - Adjectives that change meaning: listo, bueno, malo, abierto, cerrado, rico, aburrido, vivo, muerto, seguro, atento, orgulloso, delicado
    - Estar + participle as result of action

16. **Connectors and discourse markers (expanded)**
    - Cause: ya que, puesto que, dado que, como (initial position)
    - Concession: aunque, a pesar de que, sin embargo, no obstante
    - Condition: si, a no ser que, siempre que, con tal de que
    - Consequence: por eso, por lo tanto, de modo que, así que, de ahí que (+ subjunctive)
    - Addition: además, incluso, aparte de
    - Contrast: en cambio, por el contrario, mientras que

17. **Impersonal se vs. passive se**
    - se + 3rd singular (impersonal): "Se come bien aquí", "Se puede aparcar"
    - se + 3rd singular/plural (passive): "Se vende piso" / "Se venden pisos"
    - Agreement patterns and constraints

18. **Verbs of change (pseudocopulativos)**
    - ponerse + adjective (involuntary, temporary): ponerse rojo, ponerse nervioso
    - quedarse + adjective (result of process): quedarse calvo, quedarse dormido
    - hacerse + noun/adjective (voluntary, gradual): hacerse médico, hacerse rico
    - volverse + adjective (involuntary, lasting): volverse loco, volverse egoísta
    - convertirse en + noun (transformation): convertirse en un problema
    - llegar a ser + noun/adjective (achievement): llegar a ser presidente

19. **Indicative mood: expanded uses of known tenses**
    - Imperfecto of politeness: "Quería pedirte un favor"
    - Imperfecto of interrupted action (conatus): "Salía de casa cuando sonó el teléfono"
    - Present for future certainty: "Mañana salimos a las ocho"
    - Present for narrative past: "En 1492 Colón descubre América"

### Spain-Specific Notes for B1
- **Subjuntivo imperfecto -se forms**: teach both -ra and -se as equal alternatives in Spain. In exercises, accept both forms
- **Pretérito perfecto vs. indefinido**: reinforce Peninsular distribution even as narratives become more complex
- **Leísmo de persona**: continues to be standard; introduce awareness that laísmo and loísmo exist in some Peninsular dialects but are considered incorrect by the RAE
- **Probability with future/conditional**: heavily used in Spain, more so than in many Latin American varieties

## Level B2 -- Vantage / Avanzado

**Goal**: Argue, debate, express nuanced opinions. Understand complex texts. Produce clear, detailed text on a wide range of subjects. Command of the subjunctive in most contexts.

### Grammar Topics (ordered by progression)

1. **Pretérito perfecto de subjuntivo**
   - haya + past participle
   - Uses: subjunctive contexts referring to completed actions in present time frame
   - "Espero que haya llegado bien", "No creo que haya entendido", "Cuando hayas terminado, avísame"

2. **Pretérito pluscuamperfecto de subjuntivo**
   - hubiera/hubiese + past participle
   - Past unreal conditions, past regrets, past hypothetical
   - "Si hubiera sabido, no habría venido"
   - "Ojalá hubiera estudiado más"

3. **Conditional sentences type 3 (unreal past)**
   - si + pluscuamperfecto de subjuntivo, + condicional compuesto
   - "Si hubiera tenido tiempo, habría ido" / "Si hubiese tenido tiempo, habría ido"
   - Mixed conditionals: "Si hubiera estudiado, ahora tendría trabajo" (past condition, present result)
   - Colloquial substitution: "Si hubiera tenido tiempo, hubiera ido" (pluscuamperfecto in both clauses -- common in spoken Peninsular Spanish)

4. **Condicional compuesto (conditional perfect)**
   - habría + past participle
   - Past probability: "Habría llegado sobre las diez" = "He/she probably arrived around ten"
   - Unfulfilled past: "Habría querido ir pero no pude"

5. **Complete subjunctive system -- consolidation**
   - Four subjunctive tenses in full paradigm (presente, imperfecto, perfecto, pluscuamperfecto)
   - Temporal concordance (consecutio temporum) in subjunctive:
     - Main verb present > subordinate present/perfect subjunctive
     - Main verb past > subordinate imperfect/pluperfect subjunctive
   - "Quiero que vengas" vs. "Quería que vinieras"
   - "Espero que haya terminado" vs. "Esperaba que hubiera terminado"

6. **Subjunctive in concessive clauses (advanced)**
   - aunque + subjunctive (hypothetical concession): "Aunque me lo pidieras, no lo haría"
   - por mucho/más/muy que + subjunctive: "Por mucho que insistas, no cambiaré de opinión"
   - a pesar de que + subjunctive/indicative (mood determines factuality)
   - aun cuando, si bien (formal register)

7. **Subjunctive in adverbial clauses (advanced)**
   - Temporal with subjunctive (non-experienced events): "Antes de que te vayas...", "Hasta que no venga..."
   - Manner: como + subjunctive ("Hazlo como quieras")
   - Condition: a condición de que, siempre y cuando, siempre que, con tal de que (all + subjunctive)
   - Exception: a no ser que, a menos que, excepto que, salvo que (all + subjunctive)
   - Negative condition: como + subjunctive (threat): "Como no estudies, suspenderás"

8. **Indicative vs. subjunctive: nuanced contrasts**
   - Communication verbs: "Dice que viene" (reporting) vs. "Dice que venga" (ordering)
   - Negation effect: "Creo que es verdad" vs. "No creo que sea verdad", but "No digo que no sea/es verdad" (both possible with nuance)
   - Perception verbs: "Veo que estás cansado" (indicative -- fact observed) vs. "No veo que estés cansado" (subjunctive -- fact denied)
   - Emotion verbs with same-subject infinitive: "Me alegro de haber venido" vs. different-subject subjunctive: "Me alegro de que hayas venido"

9. **Advanced relative clauses**
   - el cual, la cual, los cuales, las cuales (formal, after prepositions, after commas)
   - cuyo/a/os/as (possessive relative): "El autor cuyo libro leí" -- formal but required at B2
   - lo que, lo cual (referring to entire clause): "Llegó tarde, lo cual me molestó"
   - Explanatory (non-restrictive) vs. restrictive: comma changes meaning
   - Subjunctive in relatives: "No hay nadie que pueda hacerlo" vs. "Hay alguien que puede hacerlo"

10. **Clitic pronoun sequences and advanced placement**
    - Full hierarchy: se > te > me > lo/la/le/los/las/les
    - Redundant OI: "A María le regalé un libro", "Le dije a Juan que viniera"
    - Topicalization with OD reduplication: "Los libros los tiene Juan"
    - Dative of interest: "No me le grites al niño", "Se me cayó el vaso" (involuntary events)

11. **Passive voice (advanced)**
    - ser + participle with agent (full passive): "La ley fue aprobada por el parlamento"
    - estar + participle (resultative): "La puerta está cerrada"
    - Passive reflexive constraints: not with explicit agent, agreement with patient
    - Choosing between passive constructions based on register and context

12. **Reported speech (advanced)**
    - Complex backshifting with subjunctive: "Me pidió que le ayudara" > "Me pidió que le hubiera ayudado"
    - Reporting questions: "Me preguntó si había ido" / "Me preguntó dónde vivía"
    - Reporting suggestions: "Me sugirió que fuera al médico"
    - Maintaining vs. shifting tenses based on current relevance

13. **Verbal periphrases (expanded)**
    - ir + gerund (gradual/progressive): "La situación va mejorando"
    - venir + gerund (accumulation over time): "Viene diciendo lo mismo desde hace años"
    - andar + gerund (repeated/scattered action): "Anda buscando trabajo"
    - tener + participle (accumulated result): "Tengo leídos tres libros"
    - quedar/quedarse + participle (resulting state): "El asunto quedó resuelto"
    - dar por + participle: "Doy por terminada la reunión"
    - llevar sin + infinitive: "Llevo tres días sin dormir"

14. **Advanced connector usage**
    - Cause (formal): dado que, puesto que, ya que, visto que
    - Consequence (formal): por consiguiente, en consecuencia, de ahí que (+ subjunctive)
    - Purpose: con el fin de que, con vistas a, a fin de que
    - Concession: pese a que, aun así, con todo, no obstante, si bien
    - Condition: en caso de que (+ subjunctive), de + infinitive: "De haberlo sabido, habría ido"
    - Reformulation: es decir, o sea, en otras palabras, dicho de otro modo

15. **Ser and estar: advanced and borderline cases**
    - estar + well/badly with moral judgments: "Está mal mentir"
    - ser + adjective (characterizing) vs. estar + adjective (episodic): "Juan es alegre" vs. "Juan está alegre (hoy)"
    - Recategorization: "Hoy estás muy trabajador" (unusual for you)
    - Fixed expressions: "Está claro que...", "Es evidente que..."

16. **Nominalization and word formation**
    - Infinitive as noun: "El saber no ocupa lugar"
    - lo + adjective: "lo importante", "lo bueno"
    - Suffixes: -ción, -miento, -ancia/-encia, -eza, -ura, -dad/-tad
    - Diminutives (-ito, -illo) and augmentatives (-ón, -azo) with register awareness

17. **Advanced question structures**
    - qué vs. cuál: "Qué es esto?" vs. "Cuál prefieres?" -- qué before nouns, cuál as pronoun
    - Indirect questions: "No sé si vendré", "Me pregunto cómo lo hizo"
    - Rhetorical questions: "Acaso no lo sabías?"
    - Echo questions: "Que has hecho qué?"

18. **Advanced negation**
    - ni...ni: "Ni come ni deja comer"
    - ni siquiera: "Ni siquiera me miró"
    - Expletive no: "Hasta que no venga..." (no does not negate)
    - Negative polarity items: en absoluto, en la vida, en mi vida

### Spain-Specific Notes for B2
- **Leísmo consolidation**: systematically use le for masculine person direct objects. Begin recognizing laísmo (common in Madrid/Castilla but non-standard) in listening comprehension
- **Vosotros across all subjunctive tenses**: hayáis, hubierais/hubieseis
- **Both -ra and -se forms**: at B2, students should be comfortable producing and recognizing both. Note that -se is more frequent in formal/written Peninsular Spanish
- **Colloquial conditional type 3**: "Si lo hubiera sabido, hubiera ido" (pluscuamperfecto in apodosis instead of conditional perfecto) -- common in spoken Spain Spanish

## Level C1 -- Effective Operational Proficiency / Dominio Operativo Eficaz

**Goal**: Express ideas fluently and spontaneously. Produce clear, well-structured, detailed text on complex subjects. Use language flexibly and effectively for social, academic, and professional purposes.

### Grammar Topics (ordered by progression)

1. **Subjunctive future and future perfect (futuro de subjuntivo)**
   - Forms: hablare, comiere, viviere / hubiere hablado
   - **Restricted to legal, administrative, and literary registers**: "Si alguien contraviniere esta norma...", "Sea lo que fuere"
   - Fixed expressions: "sea como fuere", "venga lo que viniere"
   - Recognizing this form is essential; productive use only in formal writing
   - **Spain-specific**: still appears in Spanish legal texts and the Constitution

2. **Pretérito anterior (hubo + participle)**
   - "Cuando hubo terminado, se fue"
   - Almost extinct in spoken Spanish; appears in literary/formal narrative
   - Replaced by pretérito indefinido or pluscuamperfecto in modern usage
   - Recognition-level knowledge; tested in DELE C1

3. **Advanced subjunctive uses**
   - Subjunctive in causals with negation: "No es que no quiera, es que no puedo"
   - Subjunctive in relatives with superlative: "Es la mejor película que haya visto"
   - Subjunctive after indefinite pronouns: "Quienquiera que sea", "Dondequiera que vaya", "Comoquiera que sea"
   - Subjunctive with como si: "Habla como si fuera español" / "como si hubiera vivido allí"
   - Independent subjunctive: "Que pase el siguiente", "Que te vaya bien"
   - Exclamative subjunctive with quién: "Quién pudiera volver a ser joven!"
   - Subjunctive with así (curse/wish): "Así te parta un rayo"

4. **Indicative/subjunctive alternation -- subtle semantic shifts**
   - "Entiendo que estás cansado" (I understand you are) vs. "Entiendo que estés cansado" (I understand that you might be -- more empathetic)
   - "Le digo que viene" (reporting) vs. "Le digo que venga" (ordering)
   - "No es que no puedo" (denial of fact) vs. "No es que no pueda" (denial of possibility) -- subjunctive is standard, indicative is emphatic
   - Perception verbs: "Veo que estás bien" (I see) vs. "No veo que estés bien" (I don't see evidence)
   - "Siento que es verdad" (I feel it is) vs. "Siento que sea verdad" (I'm sorry it is)

5. **Complex conditional structures**
   - De + infinitive (formal): "De haberlo sabido, no habría venido" = Si lo hubiera sabido...
   - Gerund as condition: "Estudiando más, aprobarás"
   - Imperative as condition: "Díselo y verás lo que pasa" = Si se lo dices...
   - Como + subjunctive (threat/warning): "Como no vengas, me enfado"
   - Suspended conditionals: "Si yo te contara..." (apodosis omitted for dramatic effect)
   - Negative conditions: a no ser que, a menos que, excepto que, salvo que
   - Remote conditions: en (el) caso de que, en el supuesto de que

6. **Topicalization and emphatic structures**
   - Left dislocation: "A mí, eso no me interesa"
   - Right dislocation: "No me interesa, eso"
   - Cleft sentences: "Lo que quiero es descansar", "Fue Juan quien lo hizo", "Es por eso por lo que no vine"
   - Focus with menudo/vaya/bonito: "Menudo lío!", "Vaya sorpresa!", "Bonito panorama!"
   - Subject inversion for focus: "Me lo dijo Juan" (not Pedro)

7. **Advanced verbal periphrases**
   - Resulting states: tener + participle ("Tengo entendido que..."), llevar + participle ("Llevo escritas diez páginas"), dejar + participle ("Lo dejé dicho")
   - Approximative: venir a + infinitive ("Viene a costar unos 50 euros")
   - Resistance/persistence: empeñarse en, insistir en, obstinarse en + infinitive
   - Imminence: estar para + infinitive, estar al + infinitive ("Está al caer")

8. **Discourse markers and connectors (C1 level)**
   - Structuring: en primer lugar, por un lado...por otro, por una parte...por otra, para empezar, en cuanto a, respecto a, en lo que se refiere a
   - Reformulation: mejor dicho, más bien, en todo caso, de todos modos
   - Exemplification: a saber, en concreto, en particular, pongamos por caso
   - Argumentation: de hecho, en realidad, en el fondo, al fin y al cabo
   - Conclusion: en definitiva, en resumidas cuentas, en suma, a fin de cuentas
   - Contact markers (oral): bueno, pues, vamos, mira, oye, fíjate

9. **Register and style variation**
   - Formal vs. informal register markers in grammar
   - Passive voice (ser + participle) as formal register marker
   - Infinitive subjects in formal style: "El consumir drogas es peligroso" (formal) vs. "Consumir drogas es peligroso" (standard)
   - Usted/ustedes register awareness in business/academic contexts even in Spain

10. **Noun phrase complexity**
    - Multiple modifiers: "las interesantes nuevas propuestas europeas de reforma"
    - Apposition (restrictive and non-restrictive): "Mi hermano Juan" vs. "Mi hermano, Juan, es ingeniero"
    - Nominalized adjectives: "los jóvenes", "lo interesante", "lo de ayer"
    - Partitive structures: "la mayoría de los estudiantes", "gran parte de la población"
    - Ad sensum agreement: "La mayoría piensa / piensan" (both correct)

11. **Advanced prepositional usage**
    - a: personal a with indefinite pronouns ("No conozco a nadie"), a with infinitive temporal value ("al llegar")
    - de: causal ("De tanto correr, se cayó"), material ("mesa de madera"), superlative ("el más listo de la clase")
    - por: concessive ("por mucho que"), distributive ("dos por persona"), means ("por correo"), causal ("por tu culpa")
    - para: evaluative ("Para ser español, habla bien inglés"), imminence ("estar para salir"), concessive ("para lo que come, está gordo")
    - Prepositional regime of verbs: comprehensive system

12. **Gerund: advanced uses and restrictions**
    - Temporal (simultaneity): "Caminando por la calle, me encontré a Juan"
    - Modal/instrumental: "Lo consiguió trabajando duro"
    - Causal: "Sabiendo la verdad, no dijo nada"
    - Predicative with perception verbs: "La vi saliendo del cine"
    - **Restrictions**: gerund cannot function as adjective modifying a noun (*"una ley regulando" is incorrect; correct: "una ley que regula")
    - Gerund of posterity (generally avoided): *"Salió cayéndose" (debated)

13. **Infinitive: advanced constructions**
    - al + infinitive (temporal): "Al enterarse, se enfadó"
    - de + infinitive (conditional, formal): "De haberlo sabido..."
    - con + infinitive (concessive): "Con ser tan listo, no aprobó"
    - Perfect infinitive: "por no haber estudiado", "después de haber cenado"
    - Infinitive in rhetorical questions: "Cómo saberlo?"

14. **Se constructions: complete system**
    - Reflexive: "Se lava" (washes himself)
    - Reciprocal: "Se quieren" (love each other)
    - Middle voice: "Se rompió el vaso" (the glass broke)
    - Impersonal: "Se vive bien aquí" (one lives well here)
    - Passive reflexive: "Se venden pisos" (flats are sold)
    - Dative of interest: "Se me olvidó" (I forgot -- involuntary)
    - Aspectual (pronominal verbs): "Se fue" vs. "Fue" (intensifying departure)
    - Ethic dative (colloquial): "Se comió tres platos" (emphatic completion)

15. **Advanced agreement patterns**
    - Collective noun agreement: "Un grupo de estudiantes aprobó/aprobaron"
    - Inclusive person: "Los españoles pensamos que..." (1st person plural with 3rd person subject)
    - Copular inversion: "Lo que más me gusta son las vacaciones" (plural verb with plural attribute)
    - Relative clause agreement: "Tú, que te crees tan listo, deberías..."

### Spain-Specific Notes for C1
- **Futuro de subjuntivo**: while archaic, it remains in Spanish legal and constitutional language -- students reading real Spanish documents will encounter it
- **-Se vs. -ra subjunctive**: in formal/academic Peninsular writing, -se forms carry a slightly more formal register. Students should alternate naturally
- **Contact markers**: bueno, pues, vamos, hombre, mujer, tío/tía -- these are deeply Peninsular conversational markers
- **Distinción in written exercises**: by C1, students should consistently distinguish c/z from s in spelling, reflecting the Peninsular phonological system (caza vs. casa, ciervo vs. siervo, cocer vs. coser)
- **Laísmo/loísmo recognition**: students should be able to identify these phenomena in authentic Peninsular texts/speech without producing them (as they are non-standard)

## Level C2 -- Mastery / Maestría

**Goal**: Understand virtually everything heard or read. Summarize information from different sources. Express ideas spontaneously, very fluently, and precisely, differentiating finer shades of meaning in complex situations.

### Grammar Topics (ordered by progression)

1. **Complete mastery of the subjunctive system**
   - All four standard tenses + futuro de subjuntivo productive use in appropriate registers
   - Nuanced mood selection with every verb type (perception, communication, cognition, emotion, influence, evaluation)
   - Subtle semantic consequences of mood choice in all clause types
   - Metalinguistic awareness: ability to explain why one mood is chosen over another

2. **Stylistic and literary grammar**
   - Historical present in narrative: "En 1808, Napoleón invade España"
   - Imperfect with future meaning in narrative: "Al día siguiente partía hacia Madrid"
   - Journalistic/narrative imperfect: "El presidente llegaba ayer a Bruselas" (instead of llegó)
   - Future of surprise/objection: "Será posible!" / "No lo harás!"
   - Conditional of objection/rejection: "Yo no diría eso"
   - Emphatic future: "Tú lo harás porque lo digo yo"

3. **Advanced conditional structures**
   - Counterfactual with implicit condition: "Yo habría aceptado" (implied: if I had been in your place)
   - Conditional perfect for past conjecture: "Habrían sido las tres cuando ocurrió"
   - Multiple conditional layers: "Si hubiera sabido que si venías me habrías ayudado, te habría llamado"
   - Periodistic conditional (unconfirmed information): "El presidente dimitiría mañana" (journalistic hedging)

4. **Cleft and pseudo-cleft constructions**
   - "Lo que pasa es que..." / "El problema es que..."
   - "Fue en Madrid donde lo conocí" (cleft with ser...donde/cuando/como/quien)
   - "Es por eso por lo que..." (reason cleft)
   - Focus and rheme manipulation for persuasive discourse

5. **Advanced relative constructions**
   - Free relatives: "Quien bien te quiere te hará llorar"
   - Generalized relatives: "Sea quien sea", "Digan lo que digan", "Cueste lo que cueste"
   - Cuyo (natural production): "El edificio cuyas ventanas están rotas..."
   - Complex prepositional relatives: "La persona en nombre de la cual hablo..."
   - Consecutive relatives: "Tiene una voz que da miedo" (so much that)

6. **Absolute constructions**
   - Participial absolute: "Terminada la reunión, todos se fueron"
   - With temporal value: "Una vez resuelta la cuestión, pasamos al siguiente punto"
   - With concessive value: "Aun admitido el error, no justifica la decisión"

7. **Advanced word order for pragmatic effect**
   - Verb-Subject for new information: "Ha llegado un paquete" (not "Un paquete ha llegado")
   - Fronting for contrast: "Eso no lo acepto yo"
   - Right-dislocation for afterthought: "No me lo creo, eso que dices"
   - Postposed subject in subordinate clauses of citation: "-- dijo María" (obligatory inversion)

8. **Superlative and intensification (advanced)**
   - Irregular absolutes: óptimo, pésimo, mínimo, máximo, ínfimo, supremo
   - Cultured forms: paupérrimo, celebérrimo, misérrimo, libérrimo
   - Prefix intensifiers: requete-, super-, ultra-, archi-, hiper-
   - Emphatic repetition: "Era grande, grande"
   - Evaluative ironic structures: "Valiente amigo!", "Menudo día!", "Bonito panorama!"

9. **Advanced copular and pseudo-copular patterns**
   - ser without adjective (distributive): "La reunión es a las tres"
   - ser (existential, philosophical): "Ser o no ser"
   - estar para (imminence): "Está para llover"
   - estar por (incomplete action / inclination): "Está por hacer" / "Estoy por no ir"
   - Pseudo-copulars: mantenerse, mostrarse, revelarse, resultar + adjective/participle
   - Recategorization: "Estás muy buen cocinero hoy" (episodic attribution)

10. **Complete prepositional system mastery**
    - Subtle distinctions: por/para in all contexts
    - Archaic/formal: cabe (near), so (under) -- literary recognition
    - Complex prepositional phrases: en virtud de, a tenor de, a merced de, en aras de, a expensas de
    - Prepositional regime: comprehensive coverage of verb + preposition collocations with semantic distinctions (soñar con vs. soñar en)

11. **Discourse coherence and cohesion**
    - Anaphoric and cataphoric reference systems
    - Ellipsis strategies: "Juan compró manzanas y Pedro [compró] peras"
    - Thematic progression patterns
    - Connector chains for extended argumentation
    - Metadiscursive commentary: "como decíamos", "volviendo al tema", "dicho esto"

12. **Punctuation-grammar interface**
    - Comma and meaning: "Los estudiantes que aprobaron..." (restrictive) vs. "Los estudiantes, que aprobaron,..." (non-restrictive)
    - Semicolon in adversative/concessive complex sentences
    - Dash for parenthetical emphasis
    - Colon before reported speech and explanation

13. **Colloquial grammar of Peninsular Spanish**
    - Ethic dative: "Se me comió tres platos", "No te me vayas"
    - Emphatic que: "Que sí, que te lo digo yo"
    - Pues as discourse filler and hesitation marker
    - Diminutives with pragmatic function: "un momentito", "ahorita" (less in Spain than Latin America, but "un ratito", "cerquita" are common)
    - Tío/tía as address form (informal, youth/adult)
    - Mola, flipa, alucina -- awareness of colloquial verb register
    - Queísmo recognition: "Me acuerdo que..." (non-standard, should be "de que")
    - Dequeísmo recognition: "Pienso de que..." (non-standard, should be "Pienso que...")

14. **Queísmo and dequeísmo**
    - Queísmo: omission of required preposition de before que ("Me alegro que vengas" -- non-standard for "Me alegro de que vengas")
    - Dequeísmo: insertion of de before que when not required ("Creo de que es verdad" -- non-standard for "Creo que es verdad")
    - Diagnostic test: substitute que-clause with "eso" -- "Me alegro de eso" (de required) vs. "Creo eso" (no de)
    - At C2, students must consistently avoid both errors and identify them in others' speech

15. **Advanced impersonal and passive constructions**
    - Impersonal se with personal a: "Se detuvo a los sospechosos"
    - Double interpretation avoidance: "Se busca secretaria" (ambiguous: passive or impersonal?)
    - Estar + being + participle (progressive passive -- rare): recognition level
    - Impersonal haber: "Hubo problemas" (not *hubieron -- common error even among natives)
    - Impersonal hacer: "Hace dos años que vivo aquí" (not *hacen)

### Spain-Specific Notes for C2
- **Full dialectal awareness within Spain**: students should recognize and understand Andalusian seseo/ceceo, yeísmo (universal in modern Peninsular Spanish except very rural areas), aspiration of final -s, loss of intervocalic -d- in participles (comío for comido -- stigmatized but frequent in colloquial speech)
- **Register mastery**: fluid movement between colloquial Peninsular, formal written, academic, legal, and literary registers
- **Laísmo/loísmo**: at C2, students must understand the geographical distribution (Central Spain for laísmo, very restricted for loísmo), sociolinguistic evaluation (both non-standard), and ability to interpret them in context
- **Distinción as productive skill**: c/z = /θ/, s = /s/ should be fully internalized for spelling; students should never confuse caza/casa, haz/has, cocer/coser, ciervo/siervo in writing
- **Queísmo/dequeísmo**: deeply understanding these phenomena as sociolinguistic variables in Peninsular Spanish
- **Native-like command of Peninsular discourse markers**: vamos, o sea, en plan, es que, bueno pues, hombre/mujer, mira, oye, fíjate que

## Summary: What Distinguishes Each Level

| Transition | Key Grammar Differentiators |
|---|---|
| A1 > A2 | Past tenses (indefinido, imperfecto), future, conditional, combined pronouns, basic subjunctive introduction |
| A2 > B1 | Systematic subjunctive (present + imperfect), conditional type 2, pluperfect, reported speech, verbal periphrases |
| B1 > B2 | Complete subjunctive system (4 tenses), conditional type 3, advanced relative clauses (cuyo, el cual), nuanced mood selection |
| B2 > C1 | Futuro de subjuntivo (recognition), advanced emphatic/topicalization structures, complete se system, register variation, discourse markers |
| C1 > C2 | Stylistic grammar, absolute constructions, native-level word order manipulation, complete dialectal awareness, colloquial grammar, error recognition (queísmo/dequeísmo) |
