# Level C2 — Mastery / Maestría

**Goal**: Understand virtually everything heard or read. Summarize information from different sources. Express ideas spontaneously, very fluently, and precisely, differentiating finer shades of meaning in complex situations.

## Grammar Topics (ordered by progression)

1. **Complete mastery of the subjunctive system**
   - All four standard tenses + futuro de subjuntivo productive use in appropriate registers
   - Nuanced mood selection with every verb type (perception, communication, cognition, emotion, influence, evaluation)
   - Subtle semantic consequences of mood choice in all clause types
   - Metalinguistic awareness: ability to explain why one mood is chosen over another

2. **Stylistic and literary grammar**
   - Historical present in narrative: "En 1808, Napoleón invade España"
   - Imperfect with future meaning in narrative: "Al día siguiente partía hacia Madrid"
   - Journalistic/narrative imperfect: "El presidente llegaba ayer a Bruselas" (instead of llegó)
   - Future of surprise/objection: "Será posible!" / "No lo harás!"
   - Conditional of objection/rejection: "Yo no diría eso"
   - Emphatic future: "Tú lo harás porque lo digo yo"

3. **Advanced conditional structures**
   - Counterfactual with implicit condition: "Yo habría aceptado" (implied: if I had been in your place)
   - Conditional perfect for past conjecture: "Habrían sido las tres cuando ocurrió"
   - Multiple conditional layers: "Si hubiera sabido que si venías me habrías ayudado, te habría llamado"
   - Periodistic conditional (unconfirmed information): "El presidente dimitiría mañana" (journalistic hedging)

4. **Cleft and pseudo-cleft constructions**
   - "Lo que pasa es que..." / "El problema es que..."
   - "Fue en Madrid donde lo conocí" (cleft with ser...donde/cuando/como/quien)
   - "Es por eso por lo que..." (reason cleft)
   - Focus and rheme manipulation for persuasive discourse

5. **Advanced relative constructions**
   - Free relatives: "Quien bien te quiere te hará llorar"
   - Generalized relatives: "Sea quien sea", "Digan lo que digan", "Cueste lo que cueste"
   - Cuyo (natural production): "El edificio cuyas ventanas están rotas..."
   - Complex prepositional relatives: "La persona en nombre de la cual hablo..."
   - Consecutive relatives: "Tiene una voz que da miedo" (so much that)

6. **Absolute constructions**
   - Participial absolute: "Terminada la reunión, todos se fueron"
   - With temporal value: "Una vez resuelta la cuestión, pasamos al siguiente punto"
   - With concessive value: "Aun admitido el error, no justifica la decisión"

7. **Advanced word order for pragmatic effect**
   - Verb-Subject for new information: "Ha llegado un paquete" (not "Un paquete ha llegado")
   - Fronting for contrast: "Eso no lo acepto yo"
   - Right-dislocation for afterthought: "No me lo creo, eso que dices"
   - Postposed subject in subordinate clauses of citation: "— dijo María" (obligatory inversion)

8. **Superlative and intensification (advanced)**
   - Irregular absolutes: óptimo, pésimo, mínimo, máximo, ínfimo, supremo
   - Cultured forms: paupérrimo, celebérrimo, misérrimo, libérrimo
   - Prefix intensifiers: requete-, super-, ultra-, archi-, hiper-
   - Emphatic repetition: "Era grande, grande"
   - Evaluative ironic structures: "Valiente amigo!", "Menudo día!", "Bonito panorama!"

9. **Advanced copular and pseudo-copular patterns**
   - ser without adjective (distributive): "La reunión es a las tres"
   - ser (existential, philosophical): "Ser o no ser"
   - estar para (imminence): "Está para llover"
   - estar por (incomplete action / inclination): "Está por hacer" / "Estoy por no ir"
   - Pseudo-copulars: mantenerse, mostrarse, revelarse, resultar + adjective/participle
   - Recategorization: "Estás muy buen cocinero hoy" (episodic attribution)

10. **Complete prepositional system mastery**
    - Subtle distinctions: por/para in all contexts
    - Archaic/formal: cabe (near), so (under) — literary recognition
    - Complex prepositional phrases: en virtud de, a tenor de, a merced de, en aras de, a expensas de
    - Prepositional regime: comprehensive coverage of verb + preposition collocations with semantic distinctions (soñar con vs. soñar en)

11. **Discourse coherence and cohesion**
    - Anaphoric and cataphoric reference systems
    - Ellipsis strategies: "Juan compró manzanas y Pedro [compró] peras"
    - Thematic progression patterns
    - Connector chains for extended argumentation
    - Metadiscursive commentary: "como decíamos", "volviendo al tema", "dicho esto"

12. **Punctuation-grammar interface**
    - Comma and meaning: "Los estudiantes que aprobaron..." (restrictive) vs. "Los estudiantes, que aprobaron,..." (non-restrictive)
    - Semicolon in adversative/concessive complex sentences
    - Dash for parenthetical emphasis
    - Colon before reported speech and explanation

13. **Colloquial grammar of Peninsular Spanish**
    - Ethic dative: "Se me comió tres platos", "No te me vayas"
    - Emphatic que: "Que sí, que te lo digo yo"
    - Pues as discourse filler and hesitation marker
    - Diminutives with pragmatic function: "un momentito", "ahorita" (less in Spain than Latin America, but "un ratito", "cerquita" are common)
    - Tío/tía as address form (informal, youth/adult)
    - Mola, flipa, alucina — awareness of colloquial verb register
    - Queísmo recognition: "Me acuerdo que..." (non-standard, should be "de que")
    - Dequeísmo recognition: "Pienso de que..." (non-standard, should be "Pienso que...")

14. **Queísmo and dequeísmo**
    - Queísmo: omission of required preposition de before que ("Me alegro que vengas" — non-standard for "Me alegro de que vengas")
    - Dequeísmo: insertion of de before que when not required ("Creo de que es verdad" — non-standard for "Creo que es verdad")
    - Diagnostic test: substitute que-clause with "eso" — "Me alegro de eso" (de required) vs. "Creo eso" (no de)
    - At C2, students must consistently avoid both errors and identify them in others' speech

15. **Advanced impersonal and passive constructions**
    - Impersonal se with personal a: "Se detuvo a los sospechosos"
    - Double interpretation avoidance: "Se busca secretaria" (ambiguous: passive or impersonal?)
    - Estar + being + participle (progressive passive — rare): recognition level
    - Impersonal haber: "Hubo problemas" (not *hubieron — common error even among natives)
    - Impersonal hacer: "Hace dos años que vivo aquí" (not *hacen)

## Spain-Specific Notes for C2
- **Full dialectal awareness within Spain**: students should recognize and understand Andalusian seseo/ceceo, yeísmo (universal in modern Peninsular Spanish except very rural areas), aspiration of final -s, loss of intervocalic -d- in participles (comío for comido — stigmatized but frequent in colloquial speech)
- **Register mastery**: fluid movement between colloquial Peninsular, formal written, academic, legal, and literary registers
- **Laísmo/loísmo**: at C2, students must understand the geographical distribution (Central Spain for laísmo, very restricted for loísmo), sociolinguistic evaluation (both non-standard), and ability to interpret them in context
- **Distinción as productive skill**: c/z = /θ/, s = /s/ should be fully internalized for spelling; students should never confuse caza/casa, haz/has, cocer/coser, ciervo/siervo in writing
- **Queísmo/dequeísmo**: deeply understanding these phenomena as sociolinguistic variables in Peninsular Spanish
- **Native-like command of Peninsular discourse markers**: vamos, o sea, en plan, es que, bueno pues, hombre/mujer, mira, oye, fíjate que
